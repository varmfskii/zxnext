General Description

The tilemap is a hardware character oriented display that comes in two resolutions: #tex2html_wrap_inline446# (#tex2html_wrap_inline448# pixels) and #tex2html_wrap_inline450# (#tex2html_wrap_inline452# pixels).

The display area on screen is the same as the sprite layer, meaning it overlaps the standard #tex2html_wrap_inline454# area by 32 pixels on all sides. Vertically this is larger than the physical HDMI display, which will cut off the top and bottom character rows making the visible area #tex2html_wrap_inline456# or #tex2html_wrap_inline458#, but the full area is visible on VGA.

The obvious application for the tilemap is for a fast, clearly readable and wide multicoloured character display. Less obvious perhaps is that it can also be used to make fast and wide resolution full colour backgrounds with easily animated components.
